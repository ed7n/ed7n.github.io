As a universal framework: {{data["unagi"].body.title}} works on most sizes, the smallest being the [<{{htmlAOut}}"https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Samsung_Galaxy_S5" >Galaxy S5];[1] it has over 24 [{%include a-page.html key="unagi_fields" html="field styles"%}][2] for both desktop- and mobile-class applications; and it employs a common design language, so switching between styles is comfortable for everyone.

Menu Bar

On top of form fields, a desktop-class menu bar is available complete with submenu, icon, and mnemonic (accelerator) display support. Its mobile-class equivalent is yet to come.

Minimum Required Widths

Using the Cantarell typeface and a global font size of 12pt, a bare presentation of {{data["unagi"].body.title}} requires a container of at least 295px wide, 313px if contained within a fieldset or likewise. An ideal presentation--like the [{%include a-page.html key="unagi_demonstration" %}]--requires a viewport of at least 340px, which is met with the Galaxy S5 (360px). These requirements can be brought down with the exclusive use of small fields and short inputs.

With the spacer-reduced mini! [{%include a-page.html key= "_about_layouts" html="layout"%}], a bare presentation of {{data["unagi" ].body.title}} requires container of at least 225px wide, 238px if contained within a fieldset or likewise.

Technologies
