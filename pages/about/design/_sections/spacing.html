We decided to space elements like in a text document. After all, this is mainly an information website. HTML is originally built for our purposes, so thankfully this isn't a difficult task to begin with.

We want the experience to be as comfortable and seamless as possible. This translates to a spacing scheme that is both easy to follow, and would not differ significantly between different screen sizes. This also means converging towards a single scheme for all screen sizes, but ours don't follow the commonplace "mobile-first" rule, as such usually ends up in excessive spacing on larger screen sizes.

We also applied an inline (side) margin on each page to limit its width. This simulates the ratio of a text document in portrait orientation, and should reduce eye movements to help the reader stay focused on the main content.

Although we use whitespace characters as spacers in some cases, we generally avoid selectable trailing and leading whitespace characters as much as possible to provide clutter-free text. You can try selecting the entire page and inspect the highlights to verify this.
