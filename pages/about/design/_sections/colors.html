Our choice of colors ensures that all elements appear to belong in their places, and that color--anything but shades of gray--is only used when necessary.

We want to put ease on the reader's eyes while maintaining adequate contrast for readability. This translates to not using a "neon" color scheme; instead, colors should not be too contrasting to cause unwanted disruption from the main content, but should differ adequately to be easily distinguishable from one another at a quick glance.

Of course, there are many other color schemes that meet the said criteria.

The Online Portal uses one of two opposing contrast schemes (light and dark)--depending on the user preference--each of which uses a similar set of colors to retain consistency in their denotations.
