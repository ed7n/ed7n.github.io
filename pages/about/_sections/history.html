The {{siteClass}} was wholly hand-coded from scratch to its launch on New Year's Eve 2020 for Mozilla Firefox, with only [{%include a-page.html key= "_2019-portal" html="one page"%}].

{{"2020-03-30" | date: formDate}}

Due to reports on missing margins with Safari and Chrome, its stylesheet was then backported to both browsers and others that did not recognize bleeding-edge definitions and short forms.

{{"2020-04-29" | date: formDate}}

As the {{siteClass}} grew, hand-coding was becoming less tolerable. So the entirety was then refactored to use [<{{htmlAOut}}"https://jekyllrb.com" >Jekyll] in a modular fashion. However, to retain its dynamicism, content modules are still mostly hand-coded.

{{"2021-01-04" | date: formDate}}

For the same reasons as above, a metadata pool was created. This allows for any page to access the metadata of any page (including itself), greatly easing maintenance. A page metadata documents a its path, title, subtitle, and those of its sections.

Lesser but cumulative changes are documented in the [{%include a-page.html key="_changes" htms=" page"%}].
